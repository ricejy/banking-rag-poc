Questions you may have

How will bonus interest be calculated?

You can earn 2 types of bonus interest:

  1. Wealth bonus
    Earn 0.45% a year for 12 consecutive months on your account average daily balance, up to the first USD 150,000 when you purchase an eligible insurance/investment products from OCBC.

  2. Top-Up Bonus
    For months when you increase your account average daily balance by at least USD 50,000 from the previous month; earn 0.70% a year on the incremental balances with no limit.

What are the eligible products to qualify for Wealth bonus?

Insurance policies (Denominated in USD only)

  • PremierLife Generation II (USD)
  • Emerald Legacy Life (USD)
  • Universal Life Plans
  • HSBC Life Variable Annuity

Investment products (Denominated in USD only)

  • Unit Trust (excluding money market funds, Systematic Investment Programme (OCBC Momentum) and Unit Trusts transferred in from other financial institutions
  • Structured deposits
  • Bonds
  • Equity-Linked Convertible Investments
  • Reverse Equity-Linked Convertible Investment
  • Structured Investments
  • Structured Notes

What happens if I subsequently purchase another eligible insurance/investment product before the completion of my current 12-month bonus period?

The Wealth bonus earned on the account will be extended for another 12 consecutive months after the effective date of (your most recent) purchase post free-look period or 14 days, whichever is later.

When will the bonus interest be paid?

Bonus Interest will be calculated at the end of each calendar month and will be credited on the 7th business day (excluding Saturday and Sunday) of the following month.

Foreign currency deposit

Foreign currency deposits are subject to exchange rate fluctuations that may provide opportunities and risks. Earnings on foreign currency deposits would be dependent on the exchanges rates prevalent at the time of their maturity if any conversion takes place. Exchange controls may be applicable from time to time to certain foreign currencies. You should therefore determine whether any foreign currency deposit is suitable for you in the light of your investment objectives, financial means and risk profile. Any pre-termination costs will be deducted from your deposit.
