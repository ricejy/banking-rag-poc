Your child’s money confidence starts here

Keep tabs on your child’s spending with the OCBC app. Get real-time alerts so you know how, when and where your child is using his/her money. No surprises here!

*Your child's other accounts, like Mighty Savers and Child Development Account, will not be shown or be available for transaction on your child's app.

Be alongside your child every step of the way

You can view your child’s transactions on your OCBC app via the parent’s dashboard. Set spending limits, schedule transfers for pocket money and receive instant notifications so that you can step in whenever.

For your peace of mind, QR cash withdrawals are limited to S$50 per day. Other transactions have a default daily limit of S$10, which can be increased after a 12-hour cooling-off period.

OCBC Kill Switch and Money Lock is available for this account. Protect your child’s money whenever you need to.

FAQs
Common questions
What services can a child access via the OCBC app?

Children will be able to do the following:

  • View account details and transactions
  • View account transactions
  • Transfer funds using PayNow
  • Make QR payments via the ‘Scan & Pay’ feature
  • Make cardless cash withdrawals at ATMs using the OCBC app
  • Activate debit card
  • Reset Card PIN
  • Lock/unlock card
  • Log in to the app using biometrics
  • Activate OCBC Money Lock
  • Activate OCBC Kill Switch
  • Access financial literacy content
  • View deals
What services can a parent of a child with an OCBC MyOwn Account access via the OCBC app?

Parents will be able to do the following:

  • Change your child's app access code
  • Set up digital access for the child
  • Link/delink the child’s mobile number to/from PayNow
  • View the child’s account details and transactions on a dashboard
  • Set up recurring allowances
  • Manage transaction limits for the child’s account and debit card
  • View the child’s account statements
  • Receive notifications on the child’s account activities

For child contact details: Please visit any OCBC Branch to update. Changes to the parent’s contact details do not automatically update the details for the OCBC MyOwn Account.

What happens when the child turns 16 – what happens to the accounts/limits/debit card?

When the child turns 16, parents will be notified and given the option to grant full control of the OCBC MyOwn Account to their child via their OCBC app. Parents have the flexibility to decide when they want to do so.

If the parent chooses to release control of the account:

  • They will have 24 hours cooling off period to rescind the request via the OCBC app
  • Once 24 hours have passed, parents will not be able to reverse this release of control process
  • Their child will then be notified that control has been passed to them, and they can log in to complete the process

After the process is completed, and the child takes over the account:

  • Parents can no longer view or get notified of transactions made on the child’s account, or set transaction limits
  • The child can change transaction limits for the account on their own, but the maximum limits on the account remain the same
  • The child can make changes on the details of the account (e.g. contact details)
  • Do note for calls into our contact centre, we can only accept instructions from the child only.

This option will be available to parents until the child turns 18. When the child turns 18, the account will be automatically converted to a FRANK Account, and parental controls will be automatically released for the child.

What can parents/children if they suspect that the account has been compromised?

Parents can choose to activate the OCBC Kill Switch via the OCBC app on the child’s behalf. To authorise this request, we will send an SMS OTP to the child’s number in our records.

Once activated, the OCBC Kill Switch will immediately suspend the child’s current and savings accounts (including joint accounts), OCBC app access and debit cards.

Children can also activate OCBC Kill Switch on their own via the OCBC app.

As a preemptive measure to protect the child’s funds, consider locking them using OCBC Money Lock. Locked funds cannot be transferred out.

What should I do if I lost my OCBC MyOwn Debit Card and/or require a card replacement?

Child can access the OCBC app > click on OCBC MyOwn Account > Lock card to temporary suspend further transactions on the card. If card is found, the child can repeat the steps to Unlock card and continue to make transactions.

Parent may contact us at 1800 363 3333 or visit any OCBC branch to request for a card replacement. You will receive it via mail within 5 working days.

If you're replacing your child's OCBC MyOwn Debit Card, the new card will feature the same design selected during account opening. Changing of card design is currently unavailable.
