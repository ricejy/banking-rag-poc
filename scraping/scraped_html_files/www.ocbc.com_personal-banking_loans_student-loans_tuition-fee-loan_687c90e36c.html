Tuition Fee Loan

Focus on what matters most – your education

  • Why you will love this
  • Who can apply
Why you will love this

Borrow up to 90% of your tuition fees

Start paying only upon graduation

Make monthly repayments from as low as S$100

Who can apply

Borrower: A Singaporean, Singapore Permanent Resident (PR) or foreigner studying at NUS, NTU, SMU or NIE

Guarantor: A Singaporean, Singapore PR or foreigner aged between 21 and 60 years old

Apply now

Pursue your education goals without financial worries.

The Tuition Fee Loan (TFL) is provided by the Ministry of Education (MOE) and administered by OCBC for NUS, NTU, NIE and SMU.

IMPORTANT NOTICE

Revision to interest rates for MOE-funded loans

From 1 April 2024, revised interest rates will apply to all new applications for MOE-funded loans. These rates will be adjusted twice a year, on 1 April and 1 October. Find out how these revised interest rates may affect your loans.

BENEFITS

Funding for your tuition fee and/or living allowance

Worried about paying for school? This loan can cover up to 90% of an MOE-subsidised tuition fee.

Zero interest incurred while you are in school

Interest charges start to apply only after you graduate1, so you can focus fully on your studies.

Flexible repayment options from just S$100 a month

Make monthly repayments of as low as S$100 after graduation.

HOW IT WORKS

Here is what you can expect with a TFL.

Step 1

Apply at any OCBC branch alongside your guarantor

Complete a TFL Application Form. Then bring it and the required documents to any OCBC branch (your guarantor must also be present).

If you are visiting our stores at NTU, NUS or SMU, please take note of the operating hours:

  • NTU: Monday to Friday, 9am to 2pm
  • NUS and SMU: Monday to Friday, 11am to 2pm

Step 2

Get your final loan details after graduation

After you graduate, we will send you a letter and email stating how you can repay your loan and when interest charges will start to apply. Find out more about government loan scheme interest rates.

Step 3

Repay your loan

If you intend to use an OCBC account for repayments, submit this Repayment Form at any OCBC branch to set up a monthly repayment plan. If you do not intend to use an OCBC account, complete and submit both the Repayment Form and GIRO Form at an OCBC branch.

The TFL is provided by the Ministry of Education (MOE) and administered by OCBC.

Eligibility requirements for borrower

Please take note of both the requirements of your respective institutions and those listed under ‘All students’.

NUS/NTU/SMU students

If you are a full-time undergraduate or postgraduate, you must be in a:

  • Full-time undergraduate and postgraduate degree programme or;
  • Graduate research programme; or
  • Full-time MOE-subsidised Master of Science (MSE) programme.

If you are a part-time undergraduate, you must be:

  • A Singapore citizen;
  • Pursuing your first undergraduate degree part-time; and
  • Pursuing a part-time course for financial reasons.

To check if you are eligible, please approach your institution of study.

NIE students

Who is eligible for the Tuition Fee Loan?

If you are an undergraduate, you must be a BA/BSc (Education) student teacher not receiving any salary or stipend, and whose fees are not paid by MOE.

If you are a graduate, please approach NIE to check if your programme is eligible.

This excludes:

  • Students receiving government/statutory board/NIE scholarships that cover tuition fees
  • International students paying non-subsidised tuition fees
  • Students receiving a full tertiary tuition fee subsidy from MENDAKI
  • Students using Central Provident Fund (CPF) savings to fully pay their subsidised tuition fees
  • Students in self-funded programmes
  • Part-time graduate students (coursework)
  • BA/BSc (Education) student teachers receiving a salary or stipend, and/or whose fees are paid by MOE
  • Students who pay full, non-subsidised fees
  • Part-time student teachers

All students

The following students will not be eligible for the TFL:

  • Students receiving government/statutory board/NIE scholarships that cover tuition fees
  • Students receiving a full tertiary tuition fee subsidy from MENDAKI
  • Students using CPF savings to fully pay their subsidised tuition fees
  • Students in self-funded graduate programmes
  • International students paying full fees (no tuition grant)

Eligibility requirements for guarantor

The guarantor must:

  • Be aged between 21 and 60 years old
  • Not be an undischarged bankrupt.

In terms of nationality:

  • If the student is Singaporean, the guarantor must be Singaporean
  • If the student is a Singapore PR, the guarantor must be a Singaporean/Singapore PR
  • If the student is a foreigner, the guarantor can be of any nationality

NOTE: You must have a guarantor with you to apply for the Tuition Fee Loan.

For selected NTU part-time and postgraduate courses. To check if your programme is eligible, please approach your institution of study.
