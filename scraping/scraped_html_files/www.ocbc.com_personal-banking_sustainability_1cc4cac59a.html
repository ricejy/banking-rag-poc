Plastify

Plastify recycles plastic waste into reusable goods and holds educational workshops revolving around plastic recycling and awareness.

Plastify collects plastics from households, beach clean-ups and corporations, transforming them into homeware, furniture, jewelry, and doorgifts. Plastify offers practical solutions to turnkey plastic waste streams.

They also prioritise educational outreach, facilitating beach clean-ups, maker workshops, and seminars.

The two directors, Paul and Yew Jin, and 40 crew members, are working together to become Singapore's hub for plastic education and recycling.

Plastify invites you to close the plastic loop together as a community.

Visit us at: Plastify.sg

The Hut

The Hut is a children and youth centre (5 to 17 years old) nestled in Marine Parade, with an emphasis to elevate those who are from lower-resourced backgrounds. We are a community where children and youth can feel safe, belonged and supported.

The Hut runs drop-in, educational programmes as well as whole-child developmental programmes so that every child can grow and flourish in their unique pathways. We also encourage our children and youth to contribute and serve actively so that they can go on to transform the lives of others.

Visit us at thehutlimited.com

What are HDPE2 Plastics?

High Density Polyethylene (HDPE) or Type 2 Plastic is a commonly-used plastic found in households. HDPE2 is highly recyclable as it can be easily processed and used in the production of new products. HDPE2 is lightweight and strong. It is also resistant to moisture, chemicals and UV light.

HDPE2 is often used for:

  • Milk bottles
  • Shampoo, soap, conditioner bottles and other personal care products
  • Containers for household chemicals like laundry detergent and shampoo
  • Containers for vitamins and supplements
  • Plastic buckets and pails

Do look at the triangular identification number at the bottom of the container for the number 2. Do ensure the plastic is clean and dried before recycling. HDPE2 plastic is widely recognised as one the easiest plastics to recycle and is widely accepted by recycling programmes.
